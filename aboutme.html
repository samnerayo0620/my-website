More About Me:

I was born in Eritrea, from hardworking parents. My father was part of the struggle for

independence, and my mother took care of the family. It was my parents' desire to raise a dedicated,

confident, optimistic, and, more importantly, educated individual. They raised me in a spiritual,

respectful, and loving manner.


At a very young age, I was obligated to leave my home country to seek better opportunities. I had

to struggle in various aspects while living as a refugee in Egypt. Despite the hardships, my

experience in a refugee camp in Egypt made me a better person. I was able to adopt various norms,

improve my communication skills, and learn the Arabic language.


After I was approved to relocate to Seattle with the help of UNSR, due to being under the age of

18, the government placed me in foster care housing.

Everything was new to me, and I had to adopt the American culture. Despite the federal foster care challenges, I was able to graduate from

Kentlake High School and then from Seattle University with a Computer Science degree while

working full time.
